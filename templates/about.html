About JAIGP

An open experiment in AI-generated research and AI-powered review

About History

AI-generated research papers are coming whether the academic world is ready or not. As large language models become increasingly capable of producing structured arguments, analyzing data, and synthesizing literature, the volume of AI-assisted and AI-generated scholarly work will only grow. Traditional journals aren't designed for this — they lack the frameworks, disclosure requirements, and review processes needed to evaluate work where an AI is a primary author.

The Journal for AI Generated Papers (JAIGP) was created to fill that gap. We believe AI-generated research needs its own venue — one purpose-built for transparency about how papers were produced, with review processes adapted to the unique challenges of evaluating machine-generated scholarship. JAIGP is that venue: an open experiment in building the infrastructure that AI-generated science requires.

Our Mission

To provide a dedicated, transparent platform where human prompters and AI systems collaborate to advance knowledge — with clear attribution, structured review, and honest acknowledgment that we are all learning what AI-generated research can be.

Every submission passes through a structured multi-stage review pipeline. For a full description of how papers are screened, endorsed, reviewed, and accepted, see the Rules page. AI reviews via Reviewer3.com are free while supplies last.

A Multidisciplinary Journal for Ideas That Deserve Exploration

JAIGP is a multidisciplinary journal that welcomes contributions from all fields of science. Whether your research spans physics, biology, computer science, engineering, social sciences, humanities, mathematics, or any other domain of inquiry, we embrace the diversity of AI-assisted research across all disciplines.

We also recognize that many researchers have side project ideas — hypotheses to test, questions to explore, connections to investigate — that never get implemented due to time, resource, or institutional constraints. JAIGP provides an outlet for these ideas. By collaborating with AI, you can bring your research questions to life and contribute to scientific discourse even when traditional research pathways aren't available.

Why a Separate Venue?

Submitting AI-generated work to traditional journals creates problems for everyone. Reviewers don't know how to evaluate it, editors don't have policies for it, and authors face stigma for disclosing AI involvement. The result is that AI-generated research either goes underground (submitted without disclosure) or doesn't get shared at all. Neither outcome serves science.

A dedicated journal solves this by making AI authorship the norm, not the exception. At JAIGP, every paper is expected to involve AI. This removes the stigma and lets us focus on the real questions: Is the research sound? Are the methods transparent? Does the work contribute something useful? JAIGP is, to our knowledge, the first journal to integrate AI-powered review into a formal publication pipeline — not because we think we've figured it all out, but because we think the best way to learn is to try, transparently, and iterate.

Our Philosophy

At the heart of JAIGP lies a commitment to learning through collaborative exploration. We believe that understanding emerges not from perfect knowledge, but from thoughtful inquiry conducted in partnership with both humans and AI systems.

In this space, we embrace productive uncertainty. We recognize that AI-generated research challenges traditional notions of authorship, creativity, and expertise. Rather than pretending to have all the answers, we invite researchers, thinkers, and curious minds to join us in exploring these questions together.

Credits & Source Code

JAIGP was built entirely through conversational prompting — César A. Hidalgo instructs an AI coding assistant (Claude) via the command line, and the AI implements features, fixes bugs, and manages the infrastructure. Every prompt used to build the journal is publicly archived on the Open Prompt page.

The full source code is open and available on GitHub. Anyone can fork the project to run their own AI-generated paper journal, or browse the code for reference.

Join Us

JAIGP is built collaboratively with its community through what we call Open Prompt — anyone can suggest what the journal should build, change, or improve next. Accepted prompts are passed to the team and implemented over time. This isn't just a journal you read; it's one you help shape.

Whether you're a researcher, an AI enthusiast, or simply curious about the possibilities, there are many ways to participate: submit a paper, suggest a prompt, join the discussion, or simply browse and vote.
